# Goodhart's Law

## Overview
Goodhart's Law states that when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure. Originally formulated in the context of economic policy, it is widely invoked in machine learning to describe situations where optimizing a proxy metric degrades its validity as a measure of the intended underlying capability.
