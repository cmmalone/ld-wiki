# AI Agent

## Overview
An AI agent is a system that pursues goals through a continuous cycle of observation, reasoning, and action — as opposed to a one-shot question-answer interaction. Agents produce real-world consequences (booked flights, sent emails) and must determine for themselves when a goal has been achieved. Multiple competing definitions exist, from Russell & Norvig's sensor/actuator framing to Anthropic's consequences-first definition.

## Across Episodes
- **S2E1**: S2E1 builds a working definition through four examples of increasing complexity, settling on the ReAct-paper framing — observe, reason, act, repeat — because the loop is what makes agents uniquely interesting and hard.
