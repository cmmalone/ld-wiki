# 45% Single-Agent Threshold

## Overview
An empirical finding from scaling law research on multi-agent systems: once a single agent can solve a given task approximately 45% of the time on its own, adding more agents to the system tends to hurt rather than help performance. Below this threshold, parallelization provides genuine gains; above it, coordination costs dominate.

## Across Episodes
- **S2E8**: S2E8 presents the 45% threshold as a practical decision rule from the Google DeepMind/MIT paper, derived from 180 configurations across four benchmarks. It is framed as an empirical regularity rather than a first-principles derivation, but useful as a design heuristic. → episodes/S2E8.md
