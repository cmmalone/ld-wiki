# The 92% Problem

## Overview
The 92% Problem refers to the finding that current AI agent benchmarks are heavily concentrated in the math and computer science domain, which represents only about 8% of the US labor market. The remaining 92% — healthcare, law, management, education, trades, hospitality, and most other fields — is dramatically under-represented in evaluation data, creating a feedback loop where agents are not benchmarked, developed, or improved for most real-world work.

## Across Episodes
- **S2E7**: S2E7 draws on a March 2026 CMU/Stanford paper that mapped 43 benchmarks and 72,000 tasks against O*NET labor market data to quantify this gap. The episode argues the concentration is driven by methodological convenience — math and code are easy to verify — not by the relative economic importance of those domains. → episodes/S2E7.md
