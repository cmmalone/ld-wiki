# Lost in the Middle Effect

## Overview
An empirically observed and mathematically proven phenomenon where large language models perform significantly worse on tasks when relevant information is positioned in the middle of a long context window, rather than near the beginning or end. Performance as a function of position follows a U-shaped curve. The effect persists regardless of context window size.
